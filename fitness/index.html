Personal Training

Endurance

As young humans, our bodies can seemingly withstand a battery of tumbles, falls and we no doubt have an endless supply of energy which keeps us going.  As we age, unless we maintain a fairly active lifestyle, our ability to endure physical exertion wanes.  This is especially true when we are overweight.

Endurance training increases the body’s ability to tolerate fatigue, stress or pain. This type of training develops cardiovascular, respiratory and muscular endurance during any aerobic or anaerobic exercise. Military training, team sports, swimming, running, cycling, weight lifting and rock climbing are just some of the types of activities that benefit from having endurance training.

As well as enhancing performance while undertaking these activities, endurance improves your ability to control your energy, contributes to your overall health, improves heart function and speeds up metabolism.

 

Flexibility

Just like endurance, we are so much more flexible when we are young. As our sense of fearlessness disappears with age, our mobility diminishes somewhat, unless we are regulars at yoga and pilates classes.

Stretching should be part of every work out, but so many people skip it, risking long term injury in the process.

Other than helping to minimise injury to the joints, including a flexibility element in your workout improves your overall strength, agility and ability to perform complex techniques that aid improved fitness.  More excitingly, it also helps tone and condition key muscle areas throughout the body.

 

HIIT

High intensity interval training has been a real buzz word in the world of fitness for some years now and for very good reason. It is any workout where you have a burst of intense activity followed by a short rest or slower pace of activity.  This cycle is alternated throughout the often short workout (Tabata, a Japanese form of HIIT lasts all of 4 minutes).  The great thing about HIIT is that rather than being another fad workout, it has been scientifically proven to burn fat quicker and more efficiently than a longer, slower workout (such as hours on various cardio machines in the gym).
